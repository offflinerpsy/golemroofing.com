Long Beach Roofing FAQ

How much does a roof replacement cost in Long Beach?

A typical residential roof replacement in Long Beach ranges from $8,000 to $30,000+, depending on roof size, material, and complexity. Larger homes in Belmont Heights or Naples with complex roof lines will be on the higher end. Smaller homes in Wrigley or Los Cerritos with standard shingle roofs start around $8,000–$12,000. We provide free, detailed estimates.

What about permits in Long Beach?

Yes, the City of Long Beach requires a building permit for roof replacements. Golem Roofing handles all permit applications, inspections, and code compliance — including Title 24 energy requirements. You don't need to visit the building department.

Do you work with homeowner insurance claims?

Yes. We regularly work with insurance companies on storm damage, leak damage, and age-related roof failures in Long Beach. We document the damage, provide detailed estimates in the format insurance adjusters require, and coordinate directly with your insurance company.

What's the best roofing material for Long Beach homes?

It depends on your home style and budget. For most Long Beach homes, we recommend GAF Timberline HDZ architectural shingles — 50-year warranty, Class A fire rating, excellent wind resistance. For Spanish-style homes, concrete tile is ideal. For flat roofs, TPO or silicone restoration. We'll help you choose during your free inspection.

How long does a roof replacement take in Long Beach?

Most residential roof replacements take 1–3 days. Larger homes or tile roofs may take 3–5 days. Long Beach's mild weather means minimal weather delays throughout the year.
